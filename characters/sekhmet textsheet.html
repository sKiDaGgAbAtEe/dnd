Personality Traits ▶
He watches over companions as if they were a litter of newborn cubs — positioning himself between danger and the vulnerable without a word. He does not show affection openly. But he is always already there.
Ideals ▶
Glory. Every battle must mean something. His name must echo in story long after his bones bleach in the sand. To be remembered is to resist erasure. Glory is not vanity — it is defiance of the forgetting.
Bonds ▶
He walks so the memory of the First Land does not die. His people are gone — faded, petrified, forgotten. He is what remains. Every step he takes is a refusal to let them disappear entirely.
Flaws ▶
He is slow to trust those outside his pride. Respect must be earned in sweat and blood — not words, not wealth, not titles. You do not enter a pride by standing near it.

In the First Land, there was no darkness — only gradients of luminance. He still reads shadow the way his ancestors read open sky.

Character Backstory
The First Land Before the Compression ▶
Long ago, his homeland was not like the world is now. The air was lighter. The horizon wider. Creatures moved as song. Spirits walked at the surface of things. There was no barrier between the mythic and the physical — they were the same substance, breathing together.
The Temporal Compression The Fall ▶
Then came the shift — not a war, not a disaster. A hardening. The world compressed. Matter thickened. What once moved like wind became stone-bound. What once sang in the air fell silent. His pride did not all survive the transition. Some faded. Some became legend. Some petrified into monuments that scholars now mistake for ancient ruins.
He Held Form Why He Survived ▶
He endured — not because he was strongest, but because his will would not dissolve. His rage anchored him to reality when reality itself was rewriting. He carries oral history that no book records. He remembers things that, to the modern world, never happened.
A World Too Small Now ▶
Now he walks a world that feels smaller than it should be. He recognizes shapes in ruins others don't. He sometimes stares at stone and sees motion. He treats mountains like former living things. He does not rant. He is steady. Grounded. A survivor of the hardening of the world.
The Core Question What drives him ▶
Does he believe the world fell once and it is done — or that the world is still falling, just slowly enough that no one else can feel it? That answer determines how he rages. And what he's really fighting for.
Additional Features & Traits
Subclass — Path of the Zealot Divine Wrath ▶
Rage is holy fury. Damage feels like divine judgment. Hard to kill — destiny-driven. Sekhmet doesn't rage because he is emotional. He rages because something sacred has been violated. The Zealot path treats survival itself as a divine mandate. Perfect for a being who refused to dissolve when everything around him did.
Origin — Tribal Nomad Outlander Background ▶
Born under a burning sun, raised among migrating prides that followed seasonal herds across vast deserts. His people valued strength, memory, and lineage above all else. They did not build monuments — they were the monument.
Appearance Notes Visual Identity ▶
Strong jaw, defined cheekbones, feline nose — human-leaning proportions. Scar over one eyebrow. Slight fang visible in a smirk. Amber eyes, slightly glowing. Thick golden-amber mane, wind-blown, darker near roots. Gold ceremonial collar and arm cuffs. Solar scar patterns across chest marking victories. One shoulder always slightly forward. He knows exactly what he is.
