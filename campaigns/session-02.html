Cold Open

A scheduling curse strikes again. One party member is unconscious somewhere — assumed to be celebrating income tax. A new face waits in the woods outside the docks, carrying letters he was supposed to deliver and a crossbow he very much will use. Somewhere offshore, skeletons are loading bodies onto a ship, and nobody has yet thought to ask why.

✦
I — The Morning After

The session opened the way the second session often does: with a DM saying it's the next day and someone saying "I forgot how to play this game." Sekhmet was absent, presumably sleeping off the goblin cave. Izabella announced she was going foraging in the woods for herbs. Evo pointed out, with mild alarm, that a fairy disappearing into the tree line was not a subtle move. He followed her anyway, from a distance, as one does when watching a potential fey incident unfold in slow motion.

Izabella rolled a 7 on Survival. She found blueberries. Nothing really significant, the DM told her. She was still in the woods, deciding what to do next, when the woods offered an answer first.

✦ ✦ ✦
II — The Bird in the Forest

He came around a tree with a crossbow drawn. A large winged figure, feathered and waiting — not ambushing, specifically, just coiled. He had been part of a merchant caravan until it was attacked and overturned, and he had retreated to the treeline to figure out his next move while staying away from whatever had done it. His name, when it came, was delivered simply.

Fable
My friends call me Fable.

Izabella, three feet tall, wings out, looked at the crossbow pointed at her and asked him to put it down. He thought about it for a few moments, then lowered it. She immediately complimented his feathers and offered to share herbs and healing potions in exchange for company. He mentioned there were bandits around. She mentioned she did not particularly care about bandits.

Evo arrived to find this exchange already in progress. He assessed the new figure, concluded the carnage at the overturned caravan was not his doing — the wounds were wrong — then pivoted immediately to asking whether Fable would like to invest in a startup. Fable did not know what a startup was. Evo explained DuckDuckDragon. Fable said he had letters to deliver and needed someone to help him figure out what had attacked the caravan. The party said they could help with that, in exchange for him joining them and helping build a wall.

He agreed. None of this was as strange to him as it probably should have been.

✦ ✦ ✦
III — First Blood: Skeletons at the Crossroads

The conversation had barely settled when the arrows came. One pierced Fable from behind before anyone had time to roll initiative. Two more found Raven and Izabella in quick succession. The skeletons had been patient.

Fable — this was his first action in the party, before he had even formally agreed to anything — pulled out a flute. He played a small tune and cast Heroism on Raven: immunity to the frightened condition, four temporary hit points at the start of each turn. Then he sent a Bardic Inspiration toward Malc. Then he moved to a less exposed position. The table noted, with some approval, that he was more of a support than a fighter in this particular moment.

Izabella opened with Ray of Frost against the Stoic Undead in the trees — a 19 plus modifier, a clean hit, 6 points of damage, the skeleton freezing mid-lurch before shattering apart. She was delighted with herself. Evo followed up with Word of Radiance, catching two targets; one failed the Constitution save and took the full hit. Raven, sneaking from cover, loosed a crossbow bolt for 12 points of damage through a skeleton that never saw her. A skeleton shortbow caught Evo for 2 on a counter. Malc whipped the final standing undead and it shatters from the intention of you wanting to hit it, the DM said, which is a generous ruling and a good one. Combat over.

Izabella had 1 HP remaining. She had been describing herself as fine the entire time. Evo healed her for 5 and said nothing about it.

✦ ✦ ✦
IV — The Docks

Three hours of travel south along the blood trail brought the party to the docks — a coastal settlement, unfamiliar, functional. Malc immediately sprinted toward the nearest building to inspect the walls. Evo followed. Izabella pointed out that there were skeletons nearby loading bodies onto a boat. This information was received as interesting but not immediately relevant to the wall inspection.

Raven slipped away and moved into the shadows. Fable closed his eyes, extended his awareness, and sent a question through the telepathic link the party had been using: is there anything you can see from over there? The answer came back through the DM: skeletons, multiple, loading bodies onto a vessel in the harbor. Raven was still in stealth. The patrol had not spotted her.

Evo's response was to pick up Malc Grann and throw him.

Evo
This is how we do it here, Fable.

Malc rolled and prevented himself from going face-first, which was the best outcome available given the circumstances. Combat began. The party was now outnumbered on a dockside with a new party member who had not yet been properly introduced to Malc's god allegations.

Evo opened with his lightning breath: a 30-foot line, Dexterity save DC 13. One skeleton rolled a 17 and took half; the other rolled a 6 and took the full 4 points of lightning damage. Both remained standing, which was disappointing. Fable fired his crossbow. Malc whipped another skeleton out of existence — it shatters from the intention, again, the DM said, and this was starting to feel like a policy. Raven, still hidden behind the building, unleashed a sneak attack for 12 points of damage on a skeleton that had no idea she was there.

Izabella used Firebolt on the remaining target, then Evo cast Toll the Dead — a Wisdom save the skeleton failed — and the last of them turned to necrotic ash. The dockside was quiet. Evo noted that there was a larger ship visible in deeper water, and that the bodies had been going somewhere specific.

The party looted the body pile. Deep inside a coffer: five silver pieces and a garnet. Raven took the garnet immediately and without discussion. Izabella announced that Raven had gotten her shiny. There was a brief territorial moment involving the silver. Nobody's hands were clean.

✦ ✦ ✦
V — The Ship and the Seal

They boarded the smaller boat and made for the larger vessel in deeper water. On the main deck: a pile of corpses ringed by something Izabella clocked immediately on an Arcana roll — a sealing spell. The kind that keeps something in, not out. Nobody had a good theory about what was being kept in. Fable pointed out that this was probably a sign to leave. The party did not leave.

Izabella
I don't know if it will be the best idea for us to try to break into this seal. What does everybody else think? Should we just kind of mind our own business until we reach safety, or should we try to break in there?

While this debate was happening, Raven had already tried to brute-force the seal. She rolled a 19 and an 18. The bodies inside the ring began to undulate. The seal had not broken entirely, but it was clearly in a worse state than before she touched it. The DM noted that the corpses were moving as if one giant mass.

Izabella stood back. She had given her opinion. The seal broke anyway.

Izabella
Raven, I fucking told you! Raven, what have you done?
Raven
Do I ever listen?

A soul erupted from the corpse. He stared at them, said hello there — interrupt my offerings, stabbed his sword into the ship's deck, and set it on fire. The bodies around him caught. The ship began to burn. He vanished.

They got back in the life raft. On the open water, with no sail and a burning ship behind them, each member of the party took 3 points of damage. Izabella had 3 HP left. She gave her last health potion to Evo, who pocketed it without drinking it. She was furious. He was unmoved.

✦ ✦ ✦
VI — The Altar and the Lich

The lifeboat deposited them somewhere new: a graveyard or courtyard or lair — the geography was unclear, but there was an altar. Blood in a pool at the center. A heart and a hand. A decayed eyeball on the rim of a well. Evo picked up the hand and chased Izabella with it. She fled across the entire platform. The hand was eventually thrown overboard.

Raven found a garnet in the blood pool — a shiny thing, naturally — and pried it loose with her knife. The altar began to glow. The heart glowed too. Blood spewed from the well area. Izabella used an 18 on Religion and identified the altar as belonging to a necromancer — a lich, specifically, one who had been sealed away from civilization and whose lair had been left forgotten for the ages.

Fable said quietly that they should probably turn around and go home.

Evo smashed the altar with his mace for 7 damage. It shattered. The blood stopped. The fog rolled in.

Across the way, the bodies began to come apart.

Illi (DM)
As you talk, the bodies across the way begin — no, not again — to come apart as a giant rips out of it as the bodies amass. Bones moving as a single creature.
Fable
Oh, bones!
Evo
I've seen this before.
✦ ✦ ✦
VII — The Bone Guardian

Initiative. Fable went first. He played another tune, cast Heroism on Malc — immunity to frightened, four temporary HP per turn — then gave Raven a Bardic Inspiration and stepped behind a pillar. Evo stepped in front of the party, raised his holy relic, and beseeched the Lord of the Walls to bless three of them. Malc, Izabella, and Raven each gained an extra d4 on their saves and attack rolls for the duration. The party was buffed. The creature was not impressed.

The bone guardian swiped at Evo for 6 damage on its first turn. Evo whipped back for 5. Raven, receiving her Bardic die, hit the creature with her bow — 11 points of damage. Izabella sent a Witch Bolt crackling into it, the lightning tethering to the giant's body. When her next turn came around, she used the sustained bolt and added a Firebolt on top of it, dropping the creature's health below halfway.

Malc held the line. The guardian attacked him twice and was parried both times. During a brief lull, a fighter's second wind brought Malc back for 6 HP — the bone teepee builder standing resolute under a hail of bone-swipes, healing himself mid-combat like a man who has been in worse rooms. Which, given the hobgoblin, he had.

Fable gave Evo the last Bardic Inspiration of the day and shot at the creature's face. Evo cast Toll the Dead; the giant failed its Wisdom save. The creature was staggering. Raven rolled 11 points of damage on her next arrow. Then Izabella drew her bow and aimed for the forehead.

Izabella
I'm going to shoot it right in the middle of the forehead of the skull.
Illi (DM)
So you pull back your bow, loose the final arrow as it hits right between the skull where the eye sockets are, and the giant begins to break apart, bones turning to ash as it vanishes.

She immediately flew across the field to hand Evo her last health potion. He looked at her, put it in his pocket, and said nothing. She stared at him for a long moment. He was already turning to look at the altar, which had begun to explode.

✦ ✦ ✦
VIII — The Lich, Briefly

The altar came apart. A figure rose from it. Tall, ancient, and unimpressed. He looked at the five of them — battered, one of them nearly out of spells, another running on 3 HP — and delivered his verdict without apparent urgency.

The Lich
None of you are worthy sacrifices yet.

He waved a hand. The party was blown back to the original town. No transition, no travel — just the harbor behind them and now a church in front of them, with a banner on its side reading The Wall, and then below that, in Evo's handwriting or close enough: DuckDuckDragon. The advertising placement had already been arranged.

The cleric at the church received them without visible surprise. He offered them rest. Izabella passed out for 48 hours. Evo constructed a teepee out of the bones he had been collecting all session and slept inside it in the middle of the church. Raven stole one of the bones from the teepee using a sleight of hand the DM described as half luck. Malc felt that something was missing but could not prove it.

The session ended there. Long rest confirmed. Level-up deferred until after stream. Nobody warned the cleric about the lich. That was a problem for next week.
